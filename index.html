Brand Story

Founders Eric and Laura Sorkin started as organic vegetable farmers but switched to maple in 2009. With Laura's culinary background and Eric's entrepreneurial skills, they created one of the first nationally recognized maple brands.

Using innovation, a keen eye for design, and an obsession for quality, they took maple syrup to places it had never been before. Today, Runamok is the nation's leading premium maple syrup brand, available in over 2,100 stores across the U.S. and internationally.

While we weren't the first to experiment with infusing and barrel-aging maple syrup, we took it further than anyone had before — from cocktails to marinades, desserts to cheese pairings, showing the world that maple syrup is one of the most versatile ingredients in the kitchen.

  • Use the "sweet" pun when appropriate
  • Speak confidently about quality and craft
Don't
  • Call products "flavored syrups" or offerings "flavors"
  • Lead with pearlescent mica in Sparkle Syrup messaging
  • Say barrel-aged products are "alcohol-free"

Catalog

Products

Always refer to products as "varieties" — never "flavors" or "flavored syrups." All maple syrups are organic unless noted. All are OU Kosher certified.

Traditional Maple

  • Sugarmaker's Cut — Pure Organic Vermont Maple. Only the peak ~10% of the season. Rich, buttery, hints of vanilla. 375ml + gallons.
  • Sugarmaker's Dark — Wood-fired, late-season. Bold caramel, butterscotch, coffee notes. 375ml.
  • Dark Robust Organic — For strong maple presence in cooking or cocktails. Gallons only.

Barrel-Aged

⚠️ Always note: "may contain trace amounts of alcohol." Never say "alcohol-free."

  • Bourbon Barrel-Aged — Caramel and vanilla notes.
  • Rum Barrel-Aged — Buttery, sweet, butter and marshmallow tones.
  • WhistlePig Whiskey Barrel-Aged — VT collaboration. 2023 Good Food Award winner.
  • Apple Brandy Barrel-Aged — Limited. Vanilla + apple. 250ml.

Sparkle Syrup

Lead with joy and applications — not the mica. If directly asked, confirm it's edible pearlescent mica.

  • Sparkle Syrup — 60ml, 250ml
  • Snow Globe Sparkle Syrup — Limited, winter
  • Star Spangled Sparkle Syrup — Limited, summer

Infused Maple

  • Cinnamon + Vanilla — "Like French toast in a bottle." Gallons + portion control.
  • Ginger — Fresh organic ginger root. Tea, pastries, cocktails.
  • Coffee — Caramel-sweet maple + coffee's chocolate and almond notes.
  • Salted Caramel — 2023 Maple Madness winner. Dairy-free caramel + Bourbon Barrel backbone. 250ml.

Smoked + Spicy

  • Hot + Spicy — Merquén chili blend. Smoky without overpowering. 250ml.
  • Smoked — Proprietary pecan wood smoking. Sweet and smoky.

Cocktail Mixers + Bitters

  • Maple Old Fashioned — Organic maple + herbs/spices. Just add bourbon.
  • Smoked Maple Old Fashioned — Pecan smoked + orange essence.
  • Maple Grenadine — No Red 40, HFCS, or artificial dyes. 250ml.
  • Aromatic Bitters — Maple base, cinnamon, clove, allspice, vanilla.
  • Orange Bitters — Citrus-forward, brightens any cocktail.
  • Floral Bitters — Cardamom, ginger, rose, citrus, clove.

Reference

FAQs

Approved answers for common customer, buyer, and press questions. Always stay on-brand — never improvise on product claims or pricing.

Are your products vegan-friendly? Product▼
Yes — all maple syrups and cocktail mixers/bitters are vegan-friendly. Exception: Wildflower Honey Infused Maple Syrup contains bee honey.
Are your products certified organic? Product▼
Almost all maple syrups, cocktail mixers, and bitters are NOFA-VT certified organic. Exceptions: Hot + Spicy Maple Syrup, Salted Caramel Infused Maple Syrup, and Sparkle Syrup.
Are your products certified Kosher? Product▼
Yes — all products are OU Kosher certified. Check the OU website for the most current listing.
Are your products gluten-free? Product▼
Organic maple syrup is naturally gluten-free, but our products and facility are not certified gluten-free. Anyone with Celiac disease or severe gluten intolerance should avoid barrel-aged syrups (aged in former spirit barrels).
Are barrel-aged syrups alcohol-free? Product▼
Never say "alcohol-free." The correct language is that barrel-aged syrups may contain trace amounts of alcohol, and may not be suitable for people who abstain from alcohol entirely.
What are the sparkles in Sparkle Syrup? Product▼
Edible sparkles. Lead with the joy and applications — don't open with "mica." If asked specifically, it's fine to confirm they're an edible pearlescent mica.
What's the shelf life? Product▼
Maple syrup: 3 years. Cocktail mixers: 2 years. Bitters: 5 years. Maple syrup won't go bad if stored properly. Refrigerate syrups and cocktail mixers after opening; bitters don't require refrigeration.
How do I know if my syrup has gone bad? Product▼
Mold typically looks like a thin white or clear film on the surface. A small amount can be fixed by skimming, re-boiling, and storing in a clean jar. Note: foam-like bubbles around the rim (especially after shipping) are normal and not spoilage.
